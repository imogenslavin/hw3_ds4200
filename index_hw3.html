I am most passionate about folk, jazz, classic rock, country rock, indie rock, and classical music. I’m classically trained in violin and have been playing since I was a kid, so music has always been central to my life and the way I connect with the world. I am currently a junior at Northeastern University studying Data Science & Public Health, with minors in Math and Music. As part of my music minor, I play in Northeastern’s orchestra, which has given me the opportunity to continue performing and to stay connected to a musical community on campus. Being surrounded by musicians with different backgrounds and styles has broadened the way I think about genre and the creative process. I am especially fascinated by music that blends influences in subtle and unexpected ways. These combinations feel emotional and familiar, yet also new. Music has always been a way for me to understand the world, express myself, and connect with others, and I love discovering how different sounds, traditions, and personal histories come together to form something unique and meaningful.

The songs currently stuck in my head are:

  • Leaving on a Jet Plane, John Denver
  • Love Takes Miles, Cameron Winter
  • Heard it in a Love Song, The Marshall Tucker Band
